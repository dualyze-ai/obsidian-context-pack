# Baroque

A dominant European art style of the 17th century. Where the Renaissance prized balance and harmony, Baroque embraced drama, intense chiaroscuro (light/shadow contrast), and emotional power. It was closely tied to the Catholic Counter-Reformation as a tool of religious persuasion.

## Key Characteristics

- Extreme contrasts of light and shadow (chiaroscuro / tenebrism)
- Dynamic movement and emotional expression
- Complex, theatrical compositions
- Religious and mythological subjects

## Leading Painters

- [[Rembrandt]] — Dutch master of "Rembrandt lighting." Night Watch.
- [[Johannes Vermeer]] — Still, intimate interiors bathed in window light. Girl with a Pearl Earring.
- [[Caravaggio]] — Italian revolutionary who pioneered tenebrism.

## Related Movements

- [[Renaissance]] — Baroque grew as a reaction against the Renaissance's classical restraint.
