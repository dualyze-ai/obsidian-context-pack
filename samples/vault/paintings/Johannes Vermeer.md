# Johannes Vermeer (1632–1675)

A [[Baroque]] master of the Dutch Golden Age. Vermeer painted domestic interior scenes — women reading letters, pouring milk, playing instruments — with an extraordinary feeling for diffused window light and quiet dignity. Only around 35 works are attributed to him, yet each is a jewel.

## Major Works

| Work | Location |
|---|---|
| Girl with a Pearl Earring | Mauritshuis, The Hague |
| The Milkmaid | Rijksmuseum, Amsterdam |
| View of Delft | Mauritshuis, The Hague |
| Woman Reading a Letter | Rijksmuseum, Amsterdam |

## Style

- Cool, even window light suffusing the scene from the left
- Pointillist-like "pearls" of highlight on surfaces
- Stillness, intimacy, and contemplative mood

## Related Artists

- [[Rembrandt]] — Dutch Golden Age contemporary. Their contrasting uses of light reward comparison.
- [[Caravaggio]] — The Baroque founding father whose dramatic light-shadow experiments echo through Vermeer.
