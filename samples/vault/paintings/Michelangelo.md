# Michelangelo (1475–1564)

One of the three great masters of the [[Renaissance]] — sculptor, painter, and architect. He considered himself primarily a sculptor, yet his Sistine Chapel ceiling stands as one of the greatest painted works in history. His heroic, muscular figures set a standard for Western art for centuries.

## Major Works

| Work | Location |
|---|---|
| Sistine Chapel ceiling ("The Creation of Adam" etc.) | Vatican Palace |
| The Last Judgment | Sistine Chapel (altar wall) |
| Doni Tondo (Holy Family) | Uffizi Gallery, Florence |

## Style

- Heroic, idealized male bodies pushed to athletic extremes
- Grand, sweeping compositions on a monumental scale
- Sculptural sense of volume brought into painting

## Related Artists

- [[Leonardo da Vinci]] — Rival genius of the same era; a productive rivalry.
- [[Raphael]] — Studied Michelangelo's powerful forms and incorporated them into his own refined style.
