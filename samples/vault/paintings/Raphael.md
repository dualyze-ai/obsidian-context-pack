# Raphael (1483–1520)

The third of the great [[Renaissance]] masters. Celebrated for grace, harmony, and idealized beauty — particularly in his Madonnas. He died at just 37, yet his output and influence on Western art were immense.

## Major Works

| Work | Location |
|---|---|
| Sistine Madonna | Gemäldegalerie Alte Meister, Dresden |
| School of Athens | Vatican Palace (Stanza della Segnatura) |
| Madonna della Seggiola | Palazzo Pitti, Florence |
| La Belle Jardinière | Musée du Louvre, Paris |

## Style

- Harmonious, balanced compositions
- Idealized, serene figures with luminous skin
- Rich color and graceful, flowing lines

## Related Artists

- [[Leonardo da Vinci]] — Raphael studied sfumato and figure composition directly from Leonardo.
- [[Michelangelo]] — Observed Michelangelo's ceiling in secret and absorbed its power into his own work.
