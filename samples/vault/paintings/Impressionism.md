# Impressionism

A revolutionary art movement that emerged in France in the 1860s–70s. Painters worked outdoors to capture fleeting light and atmosphere rather than precise detail. The name itself came from a critic mocking Monet's "Impression, Sunrise" — but the label stuck and became a badge of honor.

## Key Characteristics

- En plein air (outdoor) painting
- Loose, visible brushstrokes to capture movement and light
- Pure, unmixed colors placed side by side
- Everyday subjects: landscapes, leisure, city life

## Leading Painters

- [[Claude Monet]] — Master of the series painting. The Water Lilies define his legacy.
- [[Pierre-Auguste Renoir]] — Warm, joyful scenes of people, dances, and leisure.
- [[Edgar Degas]] — Ballet dancers, racehorses, and bold, cropped compositions.

## Related Movements

- [[Modern Art]] — Impressionism was the spark that ignited Post-Impressionism, Fauvism, and beyond.
