# Pablo Picasso (1881–1973)

The dominant force in [[Modern Art]]. Picasso co-founded Cubism — fracturing objects and figures into geometric planes seen from multiple viewpoints simultaneously — and reinvented his style repeatedly over a 75-year career. He left over 20,000 works across painting, sculpture, ceramics, and printmaking.

## Major Works

| Work | Location |
|---|---|
| Guernica | Museo Reina Sofía, Madrid |
| Les Demoiselles d'Avignon | MoMA, New York |
| Weeping Woman | Tate Modern, London |
| Blue and Rose Period works | Dispersed worldwide |

## Periods

- **Blue Period** (1901–04) — Cool blues, poverty, loneliness
- **Rose Period** (1904–06) — Warmer tones, circus performers
- **Cubism** (1907–) — Fractured multiple viewpoints on a single flat surface

## Related Artists

- [[Vincent van Gogh]] — Post-Impressionist energy that fed into Picasso's early emotional intensity.
- [[Salvador Dalí]] — Fellow Spaniard and 20th-century revolutionary, though in a very different direction.
