# Vincent van Gogh (1853–1890)

A Post-Impressionist master and central figure of [[Modern Art]]. Van Gogh used swirling, impasto brushstrokes and electrifying color contrasts to express raw emotional intensity. Almost entirely unrecognized in his lifetime, he died at 37 — and is now among the most beloved artists in history.

## Major Works

| Work | Location |
|---|---|
| The Starry Night | MoMA, New York |
| Sunflowers (multiple versions) | National Gallery, London and others |
| The Bedroom | Art Institute of Chicago and others |
| Self-portraits | Musée d'Orsay and others |
| Wheatfield with Crows | Van Gogh Museum, Amsterdam |

## Style

- Bold, swirling impasto strokes that pulse with energy
- Intense complementary color contrasts (yellow/blue, red/green)
- Over 800 letters to his brother Theo document his thoughts and struggles

## Related Artists

- [[Pablo Picasso]] — Deeply admired Van Gogh; his Expressionist energy fed into Cubism's emotional core.
- [[Salvador Dalí]] — Connected through the spirit of radical personal expression in Modern Art.
