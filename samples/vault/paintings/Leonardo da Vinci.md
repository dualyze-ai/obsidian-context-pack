# Leonardo da Vinci (1452–1519)

The supreme "Renaissance man" — painter, sculptor, architect, scientist, and inventor. He perfected the sfumato technique (smoky blurring of outlines) and studied human anatomy to achieve unmatched realism in depicting expression and movement.

## Major Works

| Work | Location |
|---|---|
| Mona Lisa | Musée du Louvre, Paris |
| The Last Supper | Santa Maria delle Grazie, Milan (mural) |
| Virgin of the Rocks | Louvre / National Gallery, London |
| Vitruvian Man | Gallerie dell'Accademia, Venice |

## Technical Innovations

- **Sfumato** — Outlines dissolved in haze, giving figures an atmospheric depth
- **Chiaroscuro** — Modelling form through the interplay of light and shadow

## Related Artists

- [[Michelangelo]] — Rival and contemporary. Each pushed the other to greater heights.
- [[Raphael]] — Younger contemporary who absorbed Leonardo's techniques and built on them.
