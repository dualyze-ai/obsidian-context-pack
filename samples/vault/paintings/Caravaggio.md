# Caravaggio (1571–1610)

The revolutionary who launched [[Baroque]] painting. Caravaggio invented tenebrism — an extreme form of chiaroscuro where figures emerge from near-total darkness — and rejected idealized beauty in favor of raw, street-level realism. His turbulent life (including a murder) became as legendary as his art.

| Work | Location |
|---|---|

## Style

- **Tenebrism** — Figures lit by a sharp, almost theatrical spotlight against near-black backgrounds
- Unidealized figures: dirty fingernails, weathered skin, ordinary people as saints
- Frozen peak moment of dramatic action

## Related Artists

- [[Rembrandt]] — The Dutch master who absorbed and elevated Caravaggio's chiaroscuro legacy.
- [[Johannes Vermeer]] — Shares the Baroque inheritance of light-as-drama, though far more quietly.
