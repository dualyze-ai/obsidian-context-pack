# Salvador Dalí (1904–1989)

The most famous face of Surrealism and a defining figure of [[Modern Art]]. Dalí rendered impossible dreamscapes with meticulous, hyper-realistic technique — creating what he called "hand-painted dream photographs." His flamboyant persona and distinctive waxed moustache made him as much a performance as a painter.

## Major Works

| Work | Location |
|---|---|
| The Persistence of Memory | MoMA, New York |

## Style

- Photo-realistic technique applied to surreal, impossible imagery
- Recurring motifs: melting clocks, elephants on stilts, lobsters
- Deeply influenced by Freudian psychoanalysis and dreams

## Related Artists

- [[Pablo Picasso]] — Fellow Spaniard and senior revolutionary whom Dalí both admired and competed with.
- [[Vincent van Gogh]] — Earlier pioneer of painting the inner world over the outer one.
