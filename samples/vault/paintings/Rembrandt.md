# Rembrandt (1606–1669)

The greatest painter of the Dutch Golden Age and one of the undisputed masters of [[Baroque]] art. Called the "master of light," he developed the signature "Rembrandt lighting" — a single shaft of light isolating the subject from deep shadow. He painted around 100 self-portraits across his lifetime.

## Major Works

| Work | Location |
|---|---|
| The Night Watch | Rijksmuseum, Amsterdam |
| The Anatomy Lesson of Dr. Nicolaes Tulp | Mauritshuis, The Hague |
| The Return of the Prodigal Son | Hermitage Museum, St Petersburg |
| Self-portraits (late period) | National Gallery, London and others |

## Style

- "Rembrandt lighting" — strong single light source against deep darkness
- Profound psychological depth in his subjects' expressions
- Self-portraits as a lifelong meditation on age and identity

## Related Artists

- [[Johannes Vermeer]] — Fellow Dutch Golden Age painter. Both masters of light, but in opposite registers.
- [[Caravaggio]] — The Italian originator of dramatic chiaroscuro who paved Rembrandt's path.
