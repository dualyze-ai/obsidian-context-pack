# Claude Monet (1840–1926)

One of the founders of [[Impressionism]]. Monet obsessively chased light — painting the same subject across different seasons and times of day in his iconic "series" works. In his final decades he created his Japanese water garden at Giverny and produced over 250 Water Lilies paintings.

## Major Works

| Work | Location |
|---|---|
| Water Lilies (large decorations) | Musée de l'Orangerie, Paris |
| Impression, Sunrise | Musée Marmottan Monet, Paris |
| Rouen Cathedral series | Musée d'Orsay and others |
| Haystacks series | Dispersed across world museums |

## Style

- Rapid brushstrokes to capture a momentary impression of light
- Minimal color mixing — pure pigments placed side by side
- Continued painting even as his eyesight failed in old age

## Related Artists

- [[Pierre-Auguste Renoir]] — Close friend and fellow outdoor painter in their early years.
- [[Edgar Degas]] — Fellow Impressionist, but preferred studio work — a striking contrast.
