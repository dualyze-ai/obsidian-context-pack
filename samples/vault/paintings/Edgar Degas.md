# Edgar Degas (1834–1917)

Associated with [[Impressionism]] but a distinctive outsider — he rarely painted outdoors, preferring the studio. Degas captured ballet dancers, racehorses, and café scenes with daring, asymmetric compositions strongly influenced by Japanese woodblock prints and photography.

## Style

- Bold, off-center cropping (influenced by Japanese ukiyo-e)
- Figures placed near the edges of the frame
- Masterful use of pastels for soft, layered texture
- Sharp eye for movement frozen in an instant

## Related Artists

- [[Claude Monet]] — Fellow Impressionist. Their approaches to light and subject could not be more different.
- [[Pierre-Auguste Renoir]] — Both painted figures, but Degas's vision is cooler and more detached.
