# Pierre-Auguste Renoir (1841–1919)

A leading figure of [[Impressionism]], celebrated for warm, luminous scenes of people at leisure — dances, boating parties, bathers. Known as "the painter of happiness," Renoir devoted his art to celebrating life's pleasures.

## Major Works

| Work | Location |
|---|---|
| Bal du moulin de la Galette | Musée d'Orsay, Paris |
| Luncheon of the Boating Party | The Phillips Collection, Washington DC |

| Large Bathers | Philadelphia Museum of Art |

## Style

- Bright, warm palette
- Softly blurred outlines, almost like memory
- Lively, natural expressions and movement in figures

## Related Artists

- [[Claude Monet]] — Lifelong friend; painted outdoors together in their youth.
- [[Edgar Degas]] — Fellow Impressionist with a shared interest in the human figure but very different methods.
