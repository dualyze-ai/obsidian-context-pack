# How Alice Turned 20 Art Notes into an AI Knowledge Base

Alice has 20 art history notes in Obsidian — painters, movements, and museum guides covering everything from Impressionism to Modern Art.

She wants ChatGPT to answer questions based on her own notes, not general knowledge.

Bob introduces a workflow using AI Context Pack.

---

## The problem: more notes, less clarity

---

**Alice:**
Hey Bob. I've been hearing about AI Context Pack. What does it actually do?

**Bob:**
Try this first. Ask ChatGPT something about your painting notes — just paste a couple of them in.

**Alice:**
Okay, I asked: "What do Van Gogh and Picasso have in common?"

It said: "Both Van Gogh and Picasso are iconic figures in Western art history who pursued revolutionary forms of expression..."

That's just... textbook stuff. ChatGPT doesn't know anything I wrote.

**Bob:**
Exactly. And I'm guessing your notes say something more specific than that.

**Alice:**
Yeah — I wrote that Van Gogh links to Modern Art, that Picasso founded Cubism and dismantled Renaissance perspective, that Impressionism was the starting point for both of them...

**Bob:**
But ChatGPT can't see any of that. It's giving you a generic answer because it doesn't have your notes.

**Alice:**
Right. So AI Context Pack just... copies my notes into ChatGPT?

**Bob:**
That's what I thought too. It's not. Let me show you.

---

## Step 1: Build the map first — AI Brief

---

**Bob:**
Don't start by sending your notes to ChatGPT. Start by understanding what's in them. Right-click on the `vault/paintings` folder and choose **Generate AI Brief**.

**Alice:**
Done. It created a file called "paintings-AI-Brief." What is this?

**Bob:**
Open it and read.

**Alice:**
"20 notes across 3 topic clusters." "Largest cluster: Modern Art, 40%." "Connectivity is low — adding links between notes would significantly improve navigability."

I've never seen a breakdown like this for my own notes.

**Bob:**
Had you actually read all 20 of them recently?

**Alice:**
Honestly, no. I knew they were there but I couldn't tell you what the overall shape was.

**Bob:**
That's what AI Brief gives you — a bird's-eye view in seconds. Which topics dominate, which connections are missing, where the gaps are.

**Alice:**
Oh, there's a Knowledge Map in here too. Impressionism and Modern Art are clustered close together. Renaissance and Baroque are grouped separately.

**Bob:**
That's the Knowledge Map — AI Brief's way of visualizing the structure it found. You can see which topics are neighbors and which ones are isolated before you do anything else.

**Alice:**
Seeing it visually makes so much more sense than reading through notes one by one.

**Bob:**
Here's the key thing though: AI Brief isn't just a report. It's the blueprint for the next step — the AI MOC.

> **💡 What is AI Brief?**
> AI Brief is an analysis summary generated by reading all your notes at once. It maps topic clusters, relationship density, connectivity, and knowledge gaps — not a copy of your content, but a structural overview. Think of it as a "health check" for your vault, and the foundation for everything that follows.

---

## Step 2: Turn the map into a table of contents — AI MOC

---

**Bob:**
Now right-click on the AI Brief file and choose **Generate AI MOC from AI Brief**.

**Alice:**
I got a file called "paintings-AI-Brief MOC." It looks like the clusters from the AI Brief turned into a table of contents. "Modern Art cluster: Van Gogh, Picasso, Dalí." "Classical cluster: da Vinci, Michelangelo, Caravaggio."

**Bob:**
That's the AI MOC.

**Alice:**
But I could make a table of contents myself. What's different here?

**Bob:**
A MOC you write yourself only shows what you were already thinking about. AI MOC is built from what the AI found — so it surfaces groupings you didn't consciously make.

**Alice:**
Hmm. I wrote Baroque and Renaissance as separate notes, but here they're grouped together as "Classical." I hadn't thought of them that way.

**Bob:**
AI Brief finds the structure. AI MOC organizes it into something you can act on. These two steps are what separate this from just copying and pasting.

> **💡 What is AI MOC?**
> MOC stands for Map of Content — essentially a structured table of contents. An AI MOC is generated automatically from AI Brief's analysis, grouping notes by topic cluster, representative notes, and relationship strength. It shows you structure that wasn't visible when you were writing individual notes.

---

## Step 3: Package everything for AI — Context Pack

---

**Alice:**
Okay, I have the AI MOC. Now what?

**Bob:**
Right-click the AI MOC and choose **Create Context Pack**. Set the output to ChatGPT.

**Alice:**
Done. It created `pack-moc-paintings-AI-Brief-MOC-chatgpt-20260613.md`. All 20 notes are in there as one document.

**Bob:**
Check the top of the file.

**Alice:**
"The following document contains source knowledge about Paintings (20 notes). Use the information in this document to answer questions. Treat the content as factual source knowledge..."

That instruction text was added automatically?

**Bob:**
Yes. And all Obsidian-specific syntax — `[[wikilinks]]`, frontmatter, comments — is cleaned out automatically. ChatGPT won't be tripped up by formatting it doesn't understand.

**Alice:**
So it's not just dumping raw notes. It's converting them into a form that AI can actually use cleanly.

> **💡 What is a Context Pack?**
> A Context Pack is a single Markdown file containing all your selected notes, cleaned and formatted for AI assistants. Obsidian-specific syntax (`[[wikilinks]]`, frontmatter, templates) is automatically removed. The result works with ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, NotebookLM, and Claude Code.

---

## Step 4: Ask — and see the difference

---

**Alice:**
Okay, I'm pasting the Context Pack into ChatGPT and asking the same question: "What do Van Gogh and Picasso have in common?"

Here's what it said:

> "Based on your notes, both Van Gogh and Picasso are central figures in your Modern Art cluster. Van Gogh started from Impressionism and pushed it toward raw emotional intensity through swirling impasto brushwork. Picasso absorbed that emotional energy and then dismantled Renaissance perspective entirely to found Cubism. What they share — according to your notes — is that both took Impressionism as their starting point and then broke entirely from what had come before. Your notes also show that Impressionism itself is the connecting thread: it appears as the origin point in both their individual note pages."

**Alice:**
That's completely different from before.

**Bob:**
How so?

**Alice:**
The first answer was just "both were revolutionary." This one explains *why* — Impressionism as the shared starting point, Van Gogh's emotional intensity feeding into Picasso's early work, Cubism as a break from Renaissance perspective. That's all in my notes.

**Bob:**
This question actually crosses three notes: the Van Gogh note, the Picasso note, and the Impressionism note. ChatGPT connected them all in one answer.

**Alice:**
If I'd tried to do that myself, I would have had to open three notes and piece it together manually. ChatGPT did it instantly — using *my* content.

**Bob:**
That's the real power of Context Pack. Not one question, one note. One question, many notes — synthesized.

## The full flow

```text
vault/paintings (20 notes)
        ↓
  Generate AI Brief
  (Analyzes structure, clusters, connectivity)
  → Knowledge Map shows topic relationships visually
  — the blueprint for your AI MOC
        ↓
  Generate AI MOC from AI Brief
  (Organizes analysis into a navigable table of contents)
  — surfaces structure you didn't consciously build
        ↓
  Create Context Pack
  (All 20 notes → 1 clean file, ready for AI)
        ↓
  ChatGPT / Claude / Gemini / NotebookLM
  (Cross-note questions, synthesis, deep dives)
```
